Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and largest city of the country. Speaking about the culture, it should be said that there are numerous parks, gardens, theatres and museums in Tokyo. Four national museums are located in Ueno Park: Tokyo National Museum, which is the country’s largest museum of traditional Japanese art, the National Museum of Western Art, Tokyo National Museum of Modern Art. There are lots of different festivals taking place in Tokyo every year such as the Sanja at Asakusa Shrine and Kanda.

Hollywood, Los Angeles, CA, USA

The place owes its name and existence to a married couple by the name of Wilcox. In 1886 Daeida and Harvey Wilcox purchased the land in the area of Los Angeles, and Daeida later decided to name it Hollywood. Then, in the early 20th century, colonel William Selig bought a piece of the land from the Wilcox couple to start his private film company. Selig was often called a pioneer of the motion picture movement in the United States, and his company was one of the first ones in Los Angeles.

Eiffel Tower, Paris, France

Paris is a capital of France. People say that it is one of the most beautiful cities in the whole world. It is also considered to be the most-populous city of France and one of the most ancient cities of Europe because it was founded in 3rd century BC by Celtic people. There are many places of Interest in Paris. People usually visit the city to see the Eiffel Tower, because it is a symbol of this city, which attracts millions of people every year. It is allowed to climb up on the top of the tower on foot or by elevator.
